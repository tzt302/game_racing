# Data and audio sources

## Circuit telemetry

The runtime circuit file `src/track/telemetry_layouts.json` is a derived,
offline dataset generated from 2025 qualifying telemetry with
[FastF1](https://github.com/theOehrly/Fast-F1), an MIT-licensed Python project.
Each circuit contains a sample approximately every five metres:

- X/Y/Z fastest-lap trajectory and elevation
- speed, RPM, gear, throttle and brake references
- Sector 1 and Sector 2 boundary locations
- reference lap and sector times

The data was generated by `tools/import_fastf1_tracks.py` from:

| Circuit | 2025 round | Reference driver | Reference lap |
| --- | ---: | --- | ---: |
| Spa-Francorchamps | 13 | NOR | 1:40.562 |
| Silverstone | 12 | VER | 1:24.892 |
| Monza | 16 | VER | 1:18.792 |
| Monaco | 8 | NOR | 1:09.954 |
| Shanghai | 2 | PIA | 1:30.641 |

Public circuit diagrams were used as a visual cross-check:

- [Spa-Francorchamps](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Spa-Francorchamps_of_Belgium.svg)
- [Silverstone](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Circuit_Silverstone_2011.svg)
- [Monza](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Monza_track_map.svg)
- [Monaco](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Circuit_Monaco.svg)
- [Shanghai](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Shanghai_International_Racing_Circuit_track_map.svg)

## Audio

Version 2.4.0 removes all runtime sound effects. Audio recordings remain only
as archived development material and are not included in the packaged game.

An unpublished V8 audio experiment used a short excerpt from the user-selected
[engine comparison video](https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV16Q4y1n7hm/) to
build six loop-safe RPM layers for local evaluation. The uploader does not
state a reusable license or identify the original YouTube recording, so these
assets must not be included in a public release without permission. Details
are recorded in `assets/audio/source/V8_SOURCE_NOTICE.md`.

Version 2.3.2 adds the real Formula 1 field recording
[F1 BR 06 Engine Starts 4.wav](https://freesound.org/people/rfhache/sounds/44765/)
by **rfhache**, licensed under
[CC BY 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/). It was recorded at
the 2006 Brazilian Grand Prix. The public preview is segmented, pitch-scaled,
normalized and crossfaded into six loop-safe RPM layers. The complete
attribution and modification notice is stored in
`assets/audio/source/ATTRIBUTION.md`.

The six RPM-layer engine loops in `assets/audio/` are adapted by **domasx2**
from a public-domain recording and published under CC0:

[OpenGameArt — racing car engine sound loops](https://opengameart.org/content/racing-car-engine-sound-loops)

Version 2.3.2 replaces the primary texture mix with the Freesound field
recording and keeps the older CC0 loops as archived source material. A quieter
runtime-generated four-stroke V6 layer preserves RPM definition. The harmonic
engine layers, turbo whistle, overrun crackle, gear-shift ignition cut, and tyre
scrub are synthesized locally and do not contain additional third-party
recordings.
